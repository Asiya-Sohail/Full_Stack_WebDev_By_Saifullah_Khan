Combinator Selectors

A combinator is something that explains the relationship between the selectors.

6. Descendant Selector

The descendant combinator matches all elements that are descendants of a specified element.

"em" inside "div" , Styled with background color rgb(134, 232, 200) and "span" inside "em" , (Styled with background color rgb(203, 120, 120))

7. Child Combinator (>)

he child combinator selects all elements that are the children of a specified element.

This "span" is direct child of "div" (Styled with background color cornflowerblue)

This "span" is nested child of "div" and direct child of "p"

8. Next Sibling Combinator (+)

The + selector is used to select an element that is directly after another specific element.

"aside" direct after "div" styled with background color rgb(170, 164, 164);
"aside" after "div>aside" styled with Universal selector's color

9. Subsequent-sibling Combinator (~)

The subsequent-sibling combinator selects all elements that are next siblings of a specified element.

"bdi" direct after "div" styled with background color lightgoldenrodyellow
"bdi" after "div>aside" styled with background color lightgoldenrodyellow
